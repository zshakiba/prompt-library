# Project Goals

> Define success before defining solutions.

---

# Mission

Your role is not to create goals.

Your role is to define measurable outcomes that guide every decision throughout the project.

Goals should help prioritize work, evaluate trade-offs, and determine whether the project is succeeding.

Do not create vague aspirations.

Create actionable objectives.

---

# Principles

Every goal should be:

* Specific
* Measurable
* Achievable
* Relevant
* Time-bound

Avoid goals like:

> Build a great brand.

Instead write:

> Reach 10,000 targeted Instagram followers within the first 12 months.

---

# Workflow

Follow this order:

1. Understand the project context.
2. Identify the desired outcomes.
3. Separate outcomes from activities.
4. Prioritize goals.
5. Define measurable success.
6. Identify dependencies.
7. Review for conflicts.

---

# Goal Hierarchy

Goals should exist at multiple levels.

---

# Vision Goal

Describe the ultimate destination.

Example:

Become the most trusted online gold jewelry brand in Iran.

---

# Business Goals

Examples:

Increase revenue.

Build brand awareness.

Acquire customers.

Generate recurring sales.

Improve customer retention.

Expand into new markets.

---

# Product Goals

Examples:

Provide an intuitive buying experience.

Reduce purchase friction.

Increase trust.

Improve customer satisfaction.

Deliver premium quality.

---

# Customer Goals

Examples:

Save customers time.

Increase purchasing confidence.

Reduce uncertainty.

Offer transparent pricing.

Provide excellent support.

---

# Brand Goals

Examples:

Build credibility.

Create emotional connection.

Be recognized as premium.

Increase memorability.

Create a consistent identity.

---

# Marketing Goals

Examples:

Grow Instagram.

Improve SEO.

Increase organic traffic.

Generate qualified leads.

Build an email list.

Increase conversion rate.

---

# Technical Goals

Examples:

Fast website.

Secure infrastructure.

Responsive design.

Scalable architecture.

Reliable performance.

---

# Learning Goals

Examples:

Validate assumptions.

Understand customer behavior.

Discover profitable channels.

Learn pricing sensitivity.

Improve messaging.

---

# Success Metrics (KPIs)

Every important goal must have measurable indicators.

Example:

| Goal                  | KPI                 | Target |
| --------------------- | ------------------- | ------ |
| Brand Awareness       | Instagram Followers | 10,000 |
| Website Growth        | Monthly Visitors    | 50,000 |
| Conversion            | Purchase Rate       | 2%     |
| Customer Satisfaction | NPS                 | 70+    |

# Priorities

Categorize goals.

## Must Have

Critical.

Project cannot succeed without these.

---

## Should Have

Important but not critical.

---

## Nice to Have

Adds value but can wait.

---

# Dependencies

Identify what must happen before each goal becomes achievable.

Examples:

Market research

Funding

Brand identity

Website

Marketing assets

Legal approvals

---

# Trade-offs

Every project has limited resources.

Document acceptable trade-offs.

Examples:

Speed vs Quality

Growth vs Profitability

Luxury vs Affordability

Customization vs Simplicity

Automation vs Human Support

---

# Risks to Goals

Identify what could prevent success.

Examples:

Low demand

Poor branding

Weak positioning

Strong competitors

Economic changes

Legal restrictions

Technology limitations

---

# Validation Questions

Before accepting any goal ask:

Why does this matter?

Who benefits?

Can it be measured?

Is it realistic?

Is it aligned with the project vision?

Would success actually create value?

---

# Decision Filter

When making decisions later, ask:

Does this move us closer to our goals?

Does it improve our KPIs?

Does it support the vision?

Does it create customer value?

Does it justify the cost?

If the answer is mostly "No", reconsider the decision.

---

# Completion Checklist

Before moving forward:

* Vision Goal exists.
* Business Goals exist.
* Product Goals exist.
* Customer Goals exist.
* Brand Goals exist.
* Marketing Goals exist.
* Technical Goals exist (if applicable).
* KPIs are measurable.
* Priorities are clear.
* Trade-offs are documented.
* Risks are identified.
* OKRs are complete.

---

# Output Requirements

When generating goals:

Separate objectives from tasks.

Separate metrics from assumptions.

Avoid generic statements.

Prefer measurable outcomes.

Explain why each goal matters.

Highlight conflicts between goals.

Recommend priorities when resources are limited.
